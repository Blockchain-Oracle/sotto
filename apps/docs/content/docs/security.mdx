## The key never enters Sotto

The payer key lives in a wallet process outside the Sotto application — your own
Canton wallet through the wallet-neutral connector, or the hosted reference
wallet (`apps/signer`), which is itself a separate process with an owner-only
key store. In the recorded DevNet purchases the application received **only a
verified signature**; it held no payer key and no payer `actAs` authority, and a
matched prepare-only control proved the agent could not prepare the direct payer
transfer that the payer could.

## What a signature authorizes

Exactly one prepared transaction, verified before approval:

- complete decoded effects — recipient, instrument, amount, fee ceiling,
  total-debit ceiling, time bounds;
- the official Canton V2 prepared-transaction hash, recomputed independently and
  matched against the participant;
- an execute-before deadline and an approval expiry.

There is no blanket allowance, no standing session that can spend, and no
signing tool exposed to a model.

## Sessions

Wallet sessions, Sotto owner sessions, human approval, and autonomous signer
authority are distinct by design. Sotto owner sessions use replay-safe
party-control proof — a challenge signed to prove control of the owner's Canton
party — not email OTP. The phrase "this signature does not move funds" appears
only on session login, never on a payment approval, because a payment approval
_does_ move funds and is labeled as exactly that.

## Local policy is labeled local policy

Process-local guards — one-use policy files, journal file modes (`0700`
directories, `0600` records), connector-side checks — are real controls, but
they are **not** ledger enforcement, and Sotto never presents them as such.
Ledger-enforced bounds are exactly the recorded `BoundedPurchaseCapability`
semantics proven on Five North: per-call limit, remaining allowance, expiry,
revocation, and duplicate rejection, enforced by Daml on the ledger. Anything
weaker is named "local policy" wherever it appears.

## What remains open

in a deployed topology, and upstream relay equivalence are recorded as open
gates in `docs/architecture/devnet-spike-result.md`. This page will change when
