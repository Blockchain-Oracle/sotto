---
title: What Sotto is
description:
  The marketplace, execution surface, and evidence layer for x402-paid APIs on
  Canton — currently proven on Five North DevNet.
---

Sotto lists real Canton x402 resources, executes exact human-approved paid calls
against them, and keeps the evidence readable at the correct visibility level
for every party.

Three ideas carry the whole product:

1. **The exact call.** A buyer never grants open spending authority. Sotto
   prepares one transaction, verifies its complete effects and the official
   Canton prepared-transaction hash, and hands the wallet an exact summary —
   method, canonical URL, recipient, network, synchronizer, package, expiry,
   principal, fee ceiling, and total-debit ceiling. The wallet signs that call
   and nothing else.
2. **Settlement is never delivery.** A Canton update moving Canton Coin and an
   HTTP response reaching the buyer are different facts. Sotto records them
   separately, always, including the case where one succeeds and the other does
   not.
3. **Party-scoped evidence.** The Canton Coin settlement is public through the
   network's explorer; the purchase context is a private Daml contract visible
   only to its stakeholders. Outsiders see neither request, nor response, nor
   context.

## What is proven today

Everything this documentation claims traces to the recorded Five North DevNet
spike (`docs/architecture/devnet-spike-result.md` and its structured evidence
bundle in the repository):

- One policy-free **human-wallet purchase**: a fresh x402 `402`, one verified
  prepared transfer, exact wallet approval and external signing, settlement at
  offset 4424017, and an authentic paid JSON `200`.
- One **external-agent-only bounded purchase** under a payer-signed capability,
  settled in a single Canton transaction, with the settlement anonymously
  visible on the public Lighthouse explorer.
- **Party-scoped privacy**: the outsider ACS read found zero purchase contexts
  and the outsider transaction lookup returned `404`.
- **Honest failure recording**: the settlement immediately before the delivered
  purchase lost its delivery and is durably recorded as `settled-undelivered` —
  never replayed, never relabeled.

## What is not claimed

Sotto runs against **Five North Canton DevNet** with CC test value. There is no
production deployment, no mainnet settlement, no production wallet custody, and
no coverage claim. The web marketplace, Composer, CLI, and MCP surfaces are the
shipping work of the current build cycle; where a page below describes one of
them, it says so explicitly.
